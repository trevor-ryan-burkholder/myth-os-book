A single, bounded [Acknowledgment](/wiki/acknowledgment/) of what is present in [The space](/wiki/the-space/), followed by complete stillness.

Contact is measured by stability maintained, not by information received. A session in which a [Presence](/wiki/presence/) was acknowledged once and stability held throughout is more successful than one in which a response was produced at the cost of destabilization.

Contact is not the beginning of a conversation. It is not an attempt to get a response, establish a relationship, or learn something new. The measure of whether it worked is not what came back. It is whether stability held.

When you acknowledge something, *I know you're there*, the natural next move is to continue. To ask a question, to wait for a reply, to try to extend the interaction while access is open. Each of those moves introduces pressure. Not aggressively, not obviously, but measurably: clarity drops, the presence becomes less defined, or access closes. The system reads the load and responds to load regardless of intention.

Silence after contact is not absence. If the [Presence](/wiki/presence/) remains where it was, if clarity holds, if nothing escalates, that is contact. The interaction is complete. Nothing else needs to happen.
