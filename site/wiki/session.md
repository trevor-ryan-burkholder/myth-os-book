A structured period of deliberate internal work with controlled conditions.

Sessions have clear entry (detecting [The space](/wiki/the-space/)), a constrained scope of activity ([observation](/wiki/detection/), [Approach](/wiki/approach/), [Contact](/wiki/contact/), or [Influence](/wiki/influence/)), and a clear exit (before intensity increases or stability tips). Sessions are short enough to repeat easily. The quality of any individual session matters less than the [Consistency](/wiki/consistency/) of doing them.

A session that ends while stability holds leaves the system in a more accessible state than it was at the start. A session that ends after pushing past the boundary leaves it in a more defended state. The difference compounds across sessions: one direction builds [Access](/wiki/access/), the other erodes it.

The session's success is measured by the condition of the system when you leave: stable means successful, regardless of how much or how little occurred. Progress does not look like breakthrough. It looks like a session that felt unremarkable but held stability throughout, followed by another session where contact was slightly easier to establish. The improvement is in the [Baseline](/wiki/baseline/), not in the peak.

The urge to push further typically appears at the point where [stability is beginning to narrow](/wiki/stable-range/). That urge is not neutral motivation. It is the most reliable indicator that the boundary is close. Exit at the boundary, not after it.

Sessions are distinct from [real-time use](/wiki/notice-step-back-adjust/). Sessions build the [Access](/wiki/access/). Real-time application is how that access gets used in the moments that matter.
