The system described in this book is not static. The resonance vectors you identified at the start are not a permanent map. The presences you have encountered are not fixed in the forms they currently take. The space itself changes as the practice develops. Understanding how that change works prevents two specific errors: treating the system as finished when it is only settled, and trying to manage the evolution rather than observing it.

---

**How resonance vectors shift**

The resonance vectors you identified early in the practice connected you to material that was relevant and accessible at that point. They produced a strong response because there was alignment between what they represented and something already present in the system. That alignment was real. It was also specific to where the system was at the time.

As the practice develops and the system changes, as presences that were once defended become more accessible, as material that was buried begins to surface, as the overall relationship to the internal world shifts, the resonance configuration shifts with it. Symbols that produced strong responses early in the practice may produce quieter responses later. New symbols that previously registered as mild interest may begin producing genuine resonance. The system is pointing toward different territory because different territory has become relevant.

This is not arbitrary. The resonance response connects to what is present and what is currently being worked with. As the practice moves material, building familiarity with presences, reducing the defensive charge around them, opening territory that was previously inaccessible, the system updates which symbols align with the current state of the work. A resonance vector that pointed toward a heavily defended presence early in the practice may continue pointing there for years. One that pointed toward accessible material may begin pointing elsewhere once that material has been worked with enough that the alignment has shifted.

The practical implication is that the resonance vectors are worth revisiting periodically, not to redesign the system, but to check whether the current list still produces genuine responses. A vector that no longer hits with the same quality is not a failure. It is information that the territory it was pointing toward has changed. A new vector that suddenly produces a strong response is the system indicating that something new has become relevant. Both are worth noticing and neither requires immediate action.

---

**How presences change**

Presences do not resolve. They do not disappear. A presence that has been worked with across months of consistent stable contact does not cease to occupy the space or cease to generate signals. What changes is the quality of the relationship to it, and through that relationship, what the presence can do within the system.

A presence that initially produced immediate shutdown on approach, where any move toward it triggered rapid closure or withdrawal, may, after extended stable contact at appropriate distance, allow observation from closer range. The stable range has expanded. Not because the presence has changed its essential character, but because the accumulated record of contact without harm has reduced the defensive charge around it. The system has registered that approach does not automatically mean destabilization. The presence can maintain its position and its function without needing to close as completely.

This is the most significant change the practice produces at the level of presences. Not resolution, not elimination, not transformation into something different, a changed relationship that allows more stable contact than was previously available. Over a long enough period of consistent work, presences that were once completely inaccessible become approachable. Presences that were once approachable from a distance become accessible at closer range. The territory expands.

What a presence does, its function, its relationship to specific signals, its role in the system, may also shift over time, but this happens more slowly and less predictably than the expansion of the stable range. The function often becomes clearer before it changes. You observe what a presence is doing accurately enough over time that its role in the system becomes visible, and that visibility is often the precondition for the role beginning to develop.

What changes, over a long enough period of consistent work, is the character of the contact itself. The defensive charge that made early approach impossible reduces. The stable range expands. Presences that required distance to observe become accessible at closer range. The system does not become peaceful. It becomes more workable. That is the long-term trajectory, not a transformation but an accumulation of changed conditions.

---

**How the space changes**

The internal environment does not stay the same across years of practice. The overall quality of the space, its stability, its navigability, the clarity of its zones and boundaries, develops with the practice.

Early in the practice, the space is often unstable: areas that were clearly differentiated in one session blend in the next, boundaries that seemed established shift, the overall structure requires significant effort to hold. This is not a problem to be solved, it is the space before the practice has stabilized it. With consistent work, the structure becomes more durable. Less effort is required to establish it at the start of each session. What was built remains available rather than needing to be rebuilt.

The topology also develops. Areas that were indistinct early in the practice become more differentiated. New zones become accessible that were not available before. The stable areas that served as ground during early sessions may expand. The less stable areas may become more navigable as the presences within them become more familiar. None of this is designed: it emerges from the practice, the same way the presences emerged from the detection process.

---

**What to do with evolution**

The instinct when the system begins to change is to manage the change, to deliberately guide it toward a particular configuration, to try to accelerate the development, to attempt to produce the evolved form directly. That instinct should be recognized and set aside. It is the same instinct as trying to force contact with a presence that is not yet accessible. The evolution happens through the practice, not through management of the practice.

When a resonance vector stops hitting with the same quality, notice it and leave it in place for now. Do not immediately remove it or replace it. Observe whether the shift is stable, whether the reduced response persists across multiple encounters, before revising the list. A temporary reduction in resonance may indicate that the relevant material is in a different phase of accessibility rather than that the vector has become irrelevant.

When a new symbol produces a strong response that was not there before, treat it the same way you treated the original vectors: hold it, observe its quality, notice whether it persists. If it continues to hit across multiple encounters, add it. If it fades, it was situational rather than structural.

When a presence that was previously inaccessible becomes approachable, begin the approach process from the beginning, as if encountering it for the first time. The stability principles that govern first contact apply to first stable contact with a presence that was previously defended, even if you have attempted contact before. The expanded stable range is not a license to move quickly. It is an invitation to move carefully from a position that is now available.

---

**What evolution is not**

Evolution in the system is not a sign of completion. It is not evidence that the most significant work is done or that the most protected material has been reached. It is the natural development of a living system through consistent practice, the way any practice develops with enough time and enough repetition.

It is also not something to pursue. A person who tries to accelerate the evolution of their symbolic system by deliberately changing their resonance vectors, or who tries to force presences into more developed forms, is producing the same error as the person who forces contact with a defended presence. The evolution happens through correct practice. It cannot be forced without producing the same result force always produces.

The system is changing. Observe it. Work with what is present at each stage. Let the next stage emerge from the practice rather than from an idea of where the practice should lead.

That is the correct relationship to a system that is alive.
