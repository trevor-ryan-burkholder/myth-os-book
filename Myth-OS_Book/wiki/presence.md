# Presence

Something that exists in [[the-space]]. An entity, object, or quality that occupies a location and is available to notice.

A presence does not have to move, speak, or do anything. It only has to be there. Presences are distinct from [[signal|signals]]. A signal points toward a presence. A presence is what the signal is pointing at.

Presences are [[detection|detected]], not [[construction|constructed]]. They exist whether you are attending to them or not. They generate signals whether you recognize the source or not. Treating the signal without reaching the presence produces management. The source stays active.

The mind moves fast toward unification. It groups, simplifies, collapses distinct elements into one before there is enough data to know they belong together. A signal that arrives fast and sharp is not the same as one that arrives slow and heavy, even if both feel bad. Keep presences separate until they prove they match.

The question is not *what is this*. The question is *what happens when this is here*. Identity anchors. [[function|Behavior]] updates.

In IFS terms, a presence is a part. [[exile]] covers exiles. [[protector]] covers managers and firefighters. Chapters 8–10 use "parts" in the clinical DID sense. Same territory.
