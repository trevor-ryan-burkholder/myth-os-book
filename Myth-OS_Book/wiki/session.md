# Session

A structured period of deliberate internal work with controlled conditions.

Clear entry: detecting [[the-space]]. Constrained scope: [[detection|observation]], [[approach]], [[contact]], or [[influence]]. Clear exit: before intensity increases or stability tips. Short enough to repeat. The quality of any single session matters less than [[consistency]].

A session that ends while stability holds leaves the system more accessible than it was at the start. A session that ends after pushing past the boundary leaves it more defended. The difference compounds across sessions. One direction builds [[access]]. The other erodes it.

Success is measured by the condition of the system on exit. Stable is successful, regardless of how much or how little occurred. Progress does not look like breakthrough. It looks like a session that felt unremarkable but held stability, followed by another where contact was slightly easier. The improvement is in the [[baseline]], not the peak.

The urge to push further arrives at the point where [[stable-range|stability begins to narrow]]. That urge is not neutral motivation. It is the most reliable indicator that the boundary is close. Exit at the boundary. Not after it.

Sessions are distinct from [[notice-step-back-adjust|real-time use]]. Sessions build access. Real-time application uses it in the moments that matter.
