# Session

A structured period of deliberate internal work with controlled conditions.

Sessions have clear entry (detecting [[the-space]]), a constrained scope of activity ([[detection|observation]], [[approach]], [[contact]], or [[influence]]), and a clear exit (before intensity increases or stability tips). Sessions are short enough to repeat easily. The quality of any individual session matters less than the [[consistency]] of doing them.

A session that ends while stability holds leaves the system in a more accessible state than it was at the start. A session that ends after pushing past the boundary leaves it in a more defended state. The difference compounds across sessions: one direction builds [[access]], the other erodes it.

The session's success is measured by the condition of the system when you leave: stable means successful, regardless of how much or how little occurred. Progress does not look like breakthrough. It looks like a session that felt unremarkable but held stability throughout, followed by another session where contact was slightly easier to establish. The improvement is in the [[baseline]], not in the peak.

The urge to push further typically appears at the point where [[stable-range|stability is beginning to narrow]]. That urge is not neutral motivation. It is the most reliable indicator that the boundary is close. Exit at the boundary, not after it.

Sessions are distinct from [[notice-step-back-adjust|real-time use]]. Sessions build the [[access]]. Real-time application is how that access gets used in the moments that matter.
