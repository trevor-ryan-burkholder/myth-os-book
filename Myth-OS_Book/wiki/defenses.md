# Defenses

Mechanisms that block direct [[access]] to protected material. Activated by pressure, force, or premature exposure.

Defenses do not evaluate intention. They read pressure. The forward force of direct attention, direct effort, direct confrontation. Curiosity produces the same response as hostility if both are applied with force. The structures read load, not the reason for it.

This is why most approaches to internal work fail. Analysis, suppression, framework-based introspection all apply pressure to a system that closes under pressure. The result is either no access (the defenses hold) or destabilized access (the defenses are bypassed under crisis, and the material surfaces without structure holding the interaction stable). Neither is movement. The gap keeps you from the material. The flooding overwhelms you with it.

Defenses are not malfunctioning. They are doing what they were built to do. The protective system exists for reasons, often very old ones, and it will keep doing its job as long as the approach gives it cause to. This system works by giving defenses no cause to engage.

In a [[working-with-a-fragmented-system|fragmented system]], defenses are often the architecture of the fragmentation. Not gatekeepers. Load-bearing walls. You do not build a relationship with a load-bearing wall by pushing on it. You build it by demonstrating, over time, that you are not a structural threat.
