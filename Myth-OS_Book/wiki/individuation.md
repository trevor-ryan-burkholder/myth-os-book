# Individuation

From Jungian psychology. The lifelong process of psychological development toward wholeness. The gradual integration of aspects of the self that have been rejected, suppressed, or undeveloped.

Individuation is not a goal to achieve and check off. It is a direction of development that continues as long as the person is alive and growing. It is not about becoming a different person. It is about becoming more fully who you are, bringing into relationship with consciousness the material that has been operating outside of it.

The [[symbolic-evolution]] described in Chapter 19, and the long arc described in Chapter 20, are consistent with the individuation process without requiring its theoretical framework. The trajectory the practice produces, presences once defended becoming accessible, the stable range expanding, the system gradually changing as more of its material is encountered, is Jungian individuation operationalized as a practical observation.

Myth-OS does not produce individuation by design. It builds the conditions under which individuation can occur by making internal material accessible. What the practice produces, earlier [[detection]], reduced intensity, expanded [[stable-range]], presences becoming more approachable, is the ground on which deeper individuation work can eventually happen.
