# Influence

Indirect adjustment of conditions in [[the-space]] that allows the system to adapt without being forced.

Influence operates through [[distance]], position, and context. It changes the environment the system is operating in rather than acting on its outputs directly. The system moves because conditions shifted, not because it was made to. Influence is the only form of intentional change available in this practice. Control, direct intervention to produce a specific outcome, produces resistance.

The system responds to force with resistance. When you try to eliminate a signal directly, it intensifies or withdraws and returns later. The push itself is what the system responds to, not the content of what you are pushing against. Influence avoids this by changing the conditions the system is operating in.

Three levers produce reliable adjustment:

- **Distance.** Closer increases intensity, farther reduces it. A signal that feels overwhelming at close range becomes manageable when the source is perceived as farther away.
- **Position.** Where you are relative to what you are observing changes the quality of the observation. A slight shift in your position within the space can move an interaction from the edge of stability to comfortably inside it.
- **Context.** The same signal behaves differently depending on where in the space it is occurring. A signal that is intense in an unstable zone often becomes more manageable when observed from a neutral area.

The experience of influence, when it works, is quiet. Nothing dramatic happens. The signal is still present, still functional. Something about the interaction is slightly different, slightly easier to hold, slightly less consuming. That subtle shift is more durable than anything that could be forced.
