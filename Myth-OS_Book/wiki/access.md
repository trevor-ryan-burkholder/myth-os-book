# Access

Indirect interaction with protected internal states. Never direct retrieval.

The central problem the system addresses is that the mind contains material which cannot be reached by thinking about it. Between conscious attention and that material there are protective structures whose function is to prevent direct access under conditions that would destabilize the rest of the system. Those structures respond to pressure, not to intention. They close whether the pressure is hostile or curious.

This produces a specific experience that brings most people to serious internal work: knowing why something keeps happening and still not being able to interrupt it. Understanding and access are not the same thing. Insight does not produce change on its own. The two operate at different levels.

Access in the Myth-OS sense is built rather than forced. It develops through repeated stable contact with material that defenses normally protect against. Each successful interaction without destabilization registers in the system as evidence that approach does not require defense to engage. Over time the defended range narrows and the accessible range widens. Nothing is overcome. The conditions change.
