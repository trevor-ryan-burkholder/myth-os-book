# When to stop

The system is designed to be used independently. The design is deliberate. Stability-first, conservative constraints, [[minimal-input]], early exits. Calibrated for self-directed practice without clinical oversight. For most people, in most conditions, it is safe to use that way.

There are conditions under which it is not. The system works inside a [[stable-range|stable range]]. When the baseline is destabilized past a point, the range shrinks to where internal work becomes counterproductive. Reading that limit is part of using the system correctly.

---

## Conditions that require stopping

**Active suicidal ideation.** Not reduced internal work. Not more careful internal work. Stopped. The activation sessions produce in a system already at that level of distress adds load to a system already past capacity. Crisis support comes first.

**Psychotic episodes, active hallucinations, significant breaks from reality.** States where the boundary between the internal space and external reality is unclear require psychiatric evaluation before internal work continues. The internal environment this system works with is distinct from psychotic experience. The distinction requires clinical assessment to hold safely. If you are uncertain whether what you are experiencing falls into this category, that uncertainty is itself the reason to stop and seek professional consultation.

**Significant dissociative escalation.** Switching more frequent or disruptive than the typical [[baseline]], extended depersonalization, amnesia outside the normal range, identity confusion more severe than usual. Particularly relevant for DID or OSDD. Part 3 describes working inside a dissociative system during ordinary periods. It does not describe working inside one that is actively destabilizing. That requires stabilization before sessions resume.

**Active substance use that affects stable observation.** The practice depends on holding a position and observing without being pulled in. Substances that alter that capacity alter the operating condition.

**Extended periods of acute external crisis.** When daily life is genuinely unstable, when basic functioning is compromised, when the external environment is demanding full resources, depth internal work does not produce useful results. Stabilization practices, grounding, routine, external support, are appropriate. Sessions aimed at deeper access are not.

---

## The practical rule

If baseline stability outside sessions is significantly compromised, stop. Get stabilized through whatever means are available: professional support, crisis resources, changes in external circumstances. The practice will still be here.

---

## Stopping is not failure

It is accurate reading of conditions. The practice continues when the conditions for it are present. When they are not, recognizing that is the most useful thing you do.
