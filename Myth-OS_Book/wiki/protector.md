# Protector

IFS term. A part whose job is to keep [[exile|exiles]] from being triggered and their pain from surfacing.

Two types:

- **Managers** work in advance. They keep situations controlled and exiles offline. They produce the functional appearance of stability.
- **Firefighters** work after the fact. An exile gets triggered and they shut the pain down fast, often through substance use, dissociation, rage, or compulsive action.

In Myth-OS, protectors are the presences that produce closure, withdrawal, or destabilization when their territory is approached. Same structure IFS describes. Different vocabulary: [[presence|presences]] in [[the-space]].

In a [[working-with-a-fragmented-system|fragmented system]], protectors are often the architecture of the fragmentation itself. Load-bearing walls, not doors. The separation between parts is the protective function. You do not build a relationship with a load-bearing wall by pushing on it. You build it by demonstrating, over a long time, that you are not a structural threat.

The approach is the same as for any presence. Observe without forcing. [[acknowledgment|Acknowledge]] without demanding. Hold position without leaning. What changes in a fragmented system is the scale of patience and the additional [[distance]] required. Start further back than seems needed. The first sessions near a heavily defended protector may involve no [[acknowledgment]] at all. Only being present in [[the-space]] while the protector is there.

Somatically, protectors register as tightening, blocking, or a forward pressure that precedes avoidance.
