# Working with a fragmented system

The conditions under which Myth-OS is used by someone with DID, OSDD, or significant dissociative responses to trauma.

The principles do not change. [[resonance|Indirect access]], [[stable-range|stability before depth]], [[minimal-input|minimal input]], no forcing. They become more important, not less. The consequences of violating them are faster and more complete. The system switches, fragments further, or floods in ways harder to contain when the underlying structure is already separated.

What changes is the relationship between sessions, the architecture of the protector system, and the timeline.

---

## What changes

**Each session may begin from scratch.** Not because previous sessions did not happen. Because the part present may not have access to them. The opening moves — [[detection|detecting]] [[the-space]], locating the structure, checking what is present — are not formalities. They are genuine first contact with conditions that may have changed since the last session, conducted by a part that may be encountering them for the first time.

**The space looks different depending on who is in it.** Some parts access [[the-space]] as a structured environment with familiar zones and recognizable presences. Others find it darker, chaotic, less navigable. Others have no access at all. Normal for a dissociative system. Not evidence that the work is failing. Evidence of which parts have developed familiarity and which have not.

**Presences may be unknown to the part currently working.** In an integrated system, a [[presence]] encountered over multiple sessions becomes familiar. In a fragmented system, a part running a session may encounter presences other parts have worked with extensively but this part has never seen. Treat it as first [[contact]].

---

## Protectors as load-bearing walls

In a fragmented system, [[protector|protectors]] are often the reason the fragmentation exists. The separation between parts, the structural walls that keep certain material inaccessible to other parts, is itself a protective function. The system did not fragment randomly. It fragmented because the material being carried was more than a unified system held without destabilizing everything.

These protectors are not testing whether you are safe enough to let in. They are maintaining structure that does not operate on that logic. Not gatekeepers to be convinced. Load-bearing walls. You do not build a relationship with a load-bearing wall by pushing on it. You build it by demonstrating, over a long time, that you are not a structural threat.

---

## Continuity across states

The absence of automatic continuity does not make continuity impossible. It requires deliberate cultivation rather than assumption.

- **External records** of sessions. Brief notes: what part was present, what area of the space was accessible, what was encountered, what the body registered. Available across states in a way internal memory may not be.
- **[[resonance-vector|Resonance vectors]]** as cross-state anchors. Resonance operates at a level the dissociative system does not fully separate. A piece of music that produces a settling response across states is a cross-state stabilization resource.
- **[[somatic-channel|Somatic grounding]]** moves are similarly cross-state transferable. The body responds before the part present has cognitive access to the practice.

---

## Stability as the primary goal

In an integrated system, the [[stable-range|stability principles]] function as good practice. In a fragmented system, they are structural requirements. The exit criteria are not guidelines. They are stops. Rising intensity: exit. Clarity dropping: exit. Body signaling escalating pressure: exit. The urge to push arrives earlier in a dissociative system and is less reliable as an opportunity signal.

Systemic stability has an additional dimension. The system as a whole, across parts, not only the part currently active, needs to not be in active crisis, significant flooding, or escalating internal conflict. Increased switching, unusual reactivity in daily life, sleep disruption, somatic symptoms without obvious cause — often the only visible indicators that something is happening in parts not currently in contact.

---

## Real-time use

The [[notice-step-back-adjust|real-time loop]] in a fragmented system carries a second question alongside *this is that signal*: *which part am I right now, and does this part have access to the practice?* When the answer is no, the somatic step is what remains. The exhale. The contact with ground.

---

## Timeline

Progress in a fragmented system is longer and less linear than in an integrated one. Genuine development is followed by periods of regression. Holding the timeline accurately, often years rather than months, prevents the chronic overreach that comes from expecting faster progress than the system sustains.
