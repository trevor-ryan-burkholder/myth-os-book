# Symbolic evolution

The natural development of the symbolic system over time as the practice deepens.

[[resonance-vector|Resonance vectors]] shift in quality as [[presence|presences]] become more or less accessible. New symbols produce resonance that did not before. Presences change in their relationship to approach and contact. Symbolic evolution is observed, not managed. It happens through correct practice, not through deliberate redesign of the system.

Vectors that pointed toward a heavily defended presence early in the practice may continue pointing there for years. Vectors that pointed toward accessible material may begin pointing elsewhere once that material has been worked with enough that the alignment has shifted. The system is pointing toward different territory because different territory has become relevant.

Presences also change. A presence that initially produced immediate shutdown on approach, where any move toward it triggered rapid closure, may, after extended stable [[contact]] at appropriate [[distance]], allow observation from closer range. The [[stable-range]] has expanded. Not because the presence has changed its essential character, but because the accumulated record of contact without harm has reduced the defensive charge around it.

The space itself develops too. Areas that were indistinct early in the practice become more differentiated. New zones become accessible that were not available before.

The instinct when the system begins to change is to manage the change, to deliberately guide it toward a particular configuration. That instinct should be set aside. It is the same instinct as trying to force [[contact]] with a [[presence]] that is not yet accessible. Evolution happens through the practice, not through management of the practice.

The Jungian framework provides the most developed account of why this happens: see [[individuation]] and [[archetype]].
