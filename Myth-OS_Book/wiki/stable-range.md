# Stable range

The window of arousal within which the system functions. Not baseline calm. The functional zone.

The stable range is the condition under which the system stays open and accessible during a session. Inside it, interaction works. Outside it, access closes. The range expands through repeated stable contact. It contracts through destabilization.

The urge to push further arrives at the point where the range begins to narrow. That makes it an exit signal, not an invitation. The urge has the quality of opportunity. What it indicates is that intensity is rising and the range is closing. Following it confirms the instability it was signaling.

Stopping early is not leaving something unfinished. It is the correct action at the correct moment. A session that ends while the range is intact leaves the system more accessible than it was at the start. A session that ends after pushing past the boundary leaves it more defended.

In a [[working-with-a-fragmented-system|fragmented system]], the exit criteria are not guidelines. They are stops. Rising intensity: exit. Clarity dropping: exit. Body signaling escalating pressure: exit. The urge to push arrives earlier in a dissociative system and is less reliable as an opportunity signal.
