# Familiarity

The accumulated recognition of a signal or [[presence]] through repeated encounter under stable conditions.

Familiarity reduces resistance: the system allows [[access]] more readily to material it has encountered before without destabilization. Familiarity is built through repetition, not through understanding. It is the primary mechanism by which [[baseline]] forms.

A signal you have encountered fifty times registers differently than one you have encountered twice. The fifty-times signal arrives already partially labeled. You do not have to work backward from the reaction to identify what is happening. The recognition is faster because the system has already done most of that work.

Familiarity also changes [[defenses|defensive charge]]. A presence that has been approached repeatedly in stable conditions, observed without force, engaged without pressure, adjusted without demand, carries less defensive charge than one that has never been approached directly. The system records that contact with this signal does not destabilize anything. Resistance exists to prevent destabilization. When the record shows that destabilization has not occurred across repeated contact, resistance decreases.
