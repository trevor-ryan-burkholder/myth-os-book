# Familiarity

The accumulated recognition of a signal or [[presence]] through repeated encounter under stable conditions.

Familiarity reduces resistance. The system allows [[access]] more readily to material it has met before without destabilization. Familiarity is built through repetition, not through understanding. It is the primary mechanism by which [[baseline]] forms.

A signal you have met fifty times registers differently than one you have met twice. The fifty-times signal arrives partially labeled. You do not have to work backward from the reaction to identify what is happening. Recognition is faster because the system has already done most of the work.

Familiarity also changes [[defenses|defensive charge]]. A presence approached repeatedly in stable conditions, observed without force, engaged without pressure, adjusted without demand, carries less defensive charge than one never approached directly. The system records that contact with this signal does not destabilize anything. Resistance exists to prevent destabilization. When the record shows it has not occurred across repeated contact, resistance decreases.
