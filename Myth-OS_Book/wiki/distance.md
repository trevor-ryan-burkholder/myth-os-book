# Distance

The primary control lever in [[the-space]].

Closer proximity to a [[presence]] increases the intensity of its effect. Greater distance reduces it. Distance is a functional property of the internal environment, not a metaphor. A signal that feels overwhelming at close range becomes manageable when the source is perceived as farther away. You have not changed what is there. You have changed your position relative to it, which changes how much of it reaches you at once.

Distance is the most reliable form of [[influence]]. When something produces intensity that makes stable observation difficult, shifting it slightly farther away is the first and most useful adjustment. The system does not have to do anything different. Conditions change, and the system adapts on its own timeline.

In a [[working-with-a-fragmented-system|fragmented system]], distance is especially important. The starting position for approach to a defended [[protector]] is further back than feels necessary. A distance that produces no response at all in the body or in [[the-space]]. Any movement toward the protector begins from there.
