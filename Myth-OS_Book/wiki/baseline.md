# Baseline

The floor level of awareness and access available under ordinary conditions.

Baseline is established through [[consistency|consistent repetition]] and represents the minimum available when conditions are not ideal. A higher baseline means faster signal recognition, easier access to [[the-space]], and more reliable real-time use without deliberate effort. Baseline is a floor, not a ceiling. It drops under stress and rises through consistent practice.

The system adapts to what it encounters repeatedly under stable conditions. A single intense session followed by several days of nothing leaves the system in roughly the same default state it was in before. The intensity produced a temporary shift. It did not change what the system treats as normal, because normal is established by what keeps happening, not by what happened once.

Baseline forms gradually. Within weeks to months of consistent short sessions, [[detection]] becomes faster, the [[somatic-channel]] settles into [[the-space]] with less preparation, and contact that required careful setup earlier becomes available with less overhead. The marker that the system is becoming baseline is that you notice things without having tried to notice them. If you are trying to notice, the system has not yet made that move.

In a [[working-with-a-fragmented-system|fragmented system]], baseline is part-specific. The floor available to the part currently present varies. A day when the floor seems to have dropped may be a day when a less-practiced part is more active, not a sign that the practice has been undone.
