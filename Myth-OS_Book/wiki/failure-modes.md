# Failure modes

The system works because of restraint. Every step depends on keeping input low enough that the system responds on its own terms. Misuse is not random. It follows one path, and that path leads back to pressure.

Every common way the practice goes wrong is a variation of the same error: adding force to a process that stops working when forced. The forms differ. The structure is identical.

---

## The four distortions

**Over-control.** Stopping a signal, changing a presence through repeated pressure, forcing a specific outcome. A presence is noticed and the move is to change it. A signal activates and the response is to shut it down rather than observe it. The session becomes intervention instead of [[contact]]. Clarity reduces. The presence distorts or withdraws.

**Over-analysis.** Replacing observation with interpretation. Assigning meaning to every signal, building explanations for every presence. Sessions produce more sophisticated explanations and less direct contact. Presences become ideas rather than elements you can observe and approach. The model is coherent and detailed. It is also a replacement for the actual thing, which is no longer being accessed.

**Avoidance.** The least obvious because it can look like correct use. The space is being entered, sessions are being run. What is happening is the internal environment used as a substitute for external engagement rather than a support for it. Real situations get hard and instead of moving through them, you retreat into [[the-space]] to process them symbolically. The system was built to translate internal work into real-world change. Used to avoid that translation, the internal work becomes self-contained.

**[[construction|Construction]].** The hardest to detect because it does not feel like misuse. The system produces what you expect rather than revealing what is there. Sessions feel productive. The space feels rich. None of it is real. The framework becomes a projector rather than a lens.

---

## The shared signature

Across all four:

- Intensity increases rather than stabilizing.
- Sessions feel more charged over time, not less.
- Outcomes are pursued rather than observed.
- Meaning is assigned rather than allowed to emerge.
- Real situations are avoided rather than engaged.
- Material arrives with a quality of confirmation rather than discovery.

Any of these indicates that pressure has re-entered the process. The source does not matter. Pressure produces the same outcome regardless of origin.

---

## The reset

Mechanical. When any warning sign is present, the correction is the same:

- Reduce intensity.
- Stop forcing interaction.
- Return to the basic process: observe without engaging, hold the space without an agenda, make light contact and exit cleanly.

Do not try to understand what went wrong before returning to the process. Analysis of the misuse is itself over-analysis. The correction is behavioral, not conceptual.

---

## The misuse check

Five questions, applied briefly after any session that felt effortful or produced a sense of going backward:

- *Am I trying to control this?*
- *Am I adding pressure?*
- *Am I avoiding something real?*
- *Am I analyzing instead of observing?*
- *Is what I'm finding shaped by what I expected to find?*

If any answer is yes, the correction is not a new technique or a deeper insight. It is a return to observation, stability, and [[minimal-input|minimal input]]. The same conditions that made the system work in the first place.
