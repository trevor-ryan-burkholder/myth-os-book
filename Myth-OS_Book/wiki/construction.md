# Construction

Producing material in [[the-space]] that reflects expectations rather than what is present.

Constructed material has a quality of compliance. It arrives because you needed it to, shaped by what you expected or hoped to find. [[detection|Detected material]] feels more stubborn, present on its own terms, often less defined than expected.

Construction is the most persistent failure mode and the hardest to catch. It does not feel like misuse. It feels like the practice working. Sessions feel productive. The space feels rich and navigable. Presences occupy locations and behave the way you expect. None of it is real. The system was never allowed to reveal itself. You are working with a model of expectation, not contact with internal material.

Construction is not only a beginner's error. It develops in mature practice when a well-developed symbolic framework starts generating the material it expects rather than receiving what is present. The framework becomes a projector rather than a lens.

The [[somatic-channel|somatic]] marker is the most reliable indicator. Constructed material produces satisfaction, a sense of things fitting together, of having found what you were looking for. Detected material produces a more neutral quality. Presence without the rightness of confirmation.

The correction: return to the earliest steps. Enter [[the-space]] with no expectation. Ask the question once and wait without anticipating an answer. Notice what arrives with a quality of not-knowing rather than with a framework already in place.
