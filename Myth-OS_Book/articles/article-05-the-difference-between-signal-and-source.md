# The Difference Between a Signal and What's Generating It

You are in a conversation and something shifts. A tightening in the chest. A sudden urge to leave. A response forming before you have decided to respond. You are already moving before you know why.

Or you are pulled in two directions at once. Part of you moving toward something. Another part resisting. Not confusion exactly. Two separate positions being held at the same time, neither fully yours, both running.

Or someone says something ordinary and you react as if it were not ordinary. The reaction is disproportionate. You know it is disproportionate. The knowing changes nothing.

These are signals. Reactions, emotions, impulses, responses that happen before you choose them. They are the most visible part of the system.

They are not the part worth focusing on.

---

A signal points toward something. It does not replace the thing it is pointing toward.

In the space described in the previous piece, there are presences. Elements that occupy locations. They exist whether or not you are attending to them. They generate signals in daily life the way a radio tower generates transmission. The signal is what reaches you. The presence is what is sending.

This distinction is not semantic. It has direct practical consequences for why most approaches stall at the same place.

When a signal activates, the response is reasonable. Address the signal. Understand it. Regulate it. Reframe it. Suppress it. Work with it in session. All of that effort lands on the signal. The presence generating it stays exactly where it was, doing exactly what it has always done. The signal is managed temporarily. The source stays active. The same signal generates again from the same place, on the same schedule, under the same conditions.

You are not failing to work hard enough. You are working on the wrong thing.

---

You have already met this distinction outside of any formal practice.

The reaction that arrives faster than thought, before you have formed an opinion, before you have decided how you feel, is not coming from your analysis. It is coming from somewhere else. Something that already had a position on the situation. Something that has had a position on situations like this for a long time and does not wait for your input.

The conflicting pulls, wanting to move toward something while another part of you resists, feeling two incompatible things at once without being confused, are not ambivalence. Not disorder. Two distinct sources, generating two distinct signals. Each one pointing toward something separate. The conflict is real because the sources are real and they genuinely have different positions.

The version of yourself that appears under specific conditions and behaves in ways the rest of you would not choose. That version is not a lapse. It is a presence operating with its own logic, its own responses, its own relationship to the situation. The signal it generates is recognizable. The source has not yet been reached.

---

The observation that the psyche organizes into distinct functional elements is not new. Internal Family Systems is built on it. Jungian psychology is built on it. Most serious depth psychology arrives at the same place through different routes. The mind is not a single unified thing. It is a system of distinct parts, each with its own function, its own responses, its own relationship to the material the whole system carries.

I am not asking you to adopt any of those frameworks. I am asking you to notice what you have already experienced directly. The conflicting pulls are real. The reactions arriving faster than thought are coming from somewhere. The versions of yourself appearing under specific conditions are not random.

The multiplicity you notice in yourself is not disorder.

It is structure.

---

Here is why this matters practically.

A signal that is managed without reaching the presence generating it produces temporary relief at best. The signal quiets. The source stays active. When conditions shift, when pressure increases, when a similar situation recurs, when the system encounters something that activates the same material, the signal returns at the same strength. Sometimes higher, because the management created pressure that the source registered and responded to.

This is the mechanism behind the experience of working on something for years and finding it unchanged. The work was real. It landed on the signal. The presence generating the signal was never contacted. It kept doing what it was organized to do, indifferent to the work happening at the surface.

Reaching the source requires a different move than addressing the signal. Not confrontation. That produces the same defensive response the second piece described. Something more like approach. Careful, patient, low-pressure approach to something that exists in a specific location in the internal environment and can be perceived there once you know what to look for.

---

Return to the space from the previous piece.

Ask once, internally: *Is there anything here?*

Then observe without engaging. Do not move toward what you notice. Do not ask it questions. Do not try to determine what it is.

You are looking for a presence, not a signal. Something that occupies a location. It may not have a clear form. It may be a quality in a particular area, a sense that one part of the space feels different in a way that is not merely structural. It may be vague enough that you are not sure whether you are noticing it or imagining it. The uncertainty is normal. It does not need to be resolved.

Notice the body alongside. A shift in the chest or belly. A change in breath. Weight appearing where there was none. The body reads the presence of what is in the space in parallel with the imagery. Both are reading the same thing.

If something is present, notice its location. Notice the quality it carries: stable, reactive, neutral, charged. Notice what the body does in response to its proximity. Then leave it there. Do not approach yet. Do not try to make contact.

If nothing is present, that is also information about current conditions. The space does not always show what is there on the first visit. It shows what conditions currently allow.

---

Recognition is the only goal of this step.

Not definition. Not understanding. Not interaction. You are confirming that something exists in a specific location within a structured space, and that you can be near it without disrupting it.

The signal is what you have been working with. The presence is what has been generating it. The gap between those two things is where every previous approach has stopped.
