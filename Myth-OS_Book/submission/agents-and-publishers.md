# Agents and Publishers — Myth-OS

A working list of agents and publishers whose track records and stated interests align with Myth-OS. Tiered by fit, not by prestige.

**Before querying anyone on this list, verify:**

1. They are open to queries (some close intermittently).
2. Their current submission guidelines (format, exclusivity, response time).
3. Their recent sales — look at Publishers Marketplace or Manuscript Wish List to confirm they are still placing books in this space.
4. Their personal Manuscript Wish List (#MSWL on social media or Manuscript Wish List website).

Agents move agencies frequently. Always confirm current employer before sending.

## Tier 3 — Niche fits and worth knowing

Agents who handle particular angles of the book that the broader list may not:

- **Trauma-specific specialists**: Look at agents who have placed books with Bessel van der Kolk's, Peter Levine's, or Janina Fisher's publishers. Cross-reference the acknowledgments sections of those books for representation credit.
- **IFS-adjacent**: Agents who placed books with Schwartz, Anderson, Earley, or others in the IFS world. Same lookup method.
- **Memoir-with-system**: Agents who place practical books that include personal disclosure. Lori Gottlieb's agent, for instance.

## Hybrid and small-press fallbacks

If traditional submission paths do not produce an offer within 12–18 months:

- **Greenleaf Book Group** — Hybrid model. Author pays production costs, retains higher royalties, retains distribution support.
- **She Writes Press / SparkPress** — Hybrid model, primarily literary nonfiction.
- **Lioncrest Publishing / Scribe Media** — Author-services with editorial support.
- **Independent**: KDP plus IngramSpark for paperback and audio distribution. Author retains all rights and royalties; bears all marketing.

These are last-resort paths if the goal is to make the book available. They are not equivalent to a Tier 1 publisher in reach.

## Cross-reference method

The most reliable way to extend this list:

1. Open the acknowledgments of *The Body Keeps the Score*, *Self-Therapy*, *Waking the Tiger*, *It Didn't Start with You*, and one or two IFS practitioner books.
2. Find the agent credit ("My agent, X, at Y agency, …").
3. Look up that agent's current employer and submission status.
4. Add to the list if open and active.

The acknowledgments approach surfaces real agents who have actually placed books in this exact space, which is more useful than directories.

---

## What to track per agent

For each query you send, record:

- Date queried
- Material sent (query only, query + sample, query + proposal)
- Personalization used (what you said about why this agent)
- Response (none, form rejection, partial request, full request, offer)
- Date of response
- Whether the agent gave any feedback worth noting

A simple spreadsheet keeps the rounds organized and prevents accidentally re-querying someone who passed.
