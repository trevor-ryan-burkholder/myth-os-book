A structured period of deliberate internal work with controlled conditions.

Sessions have clear entry (detecting [The space]({{ '/wiki/the-space/' | relative_url }})), a constrained scope of activity ([observation]({{ '/wiki/detection/' | relative_url }}), [Approach]({{ '/wiki/approach/' | relative_url }}), [Contact]({{ '/wiki/contact/' | relative_url }}), or [Influence]({{ '/wiki/influence/' | relative_url }})), and a clear exit (before intensity increases or stability tips). Sessions are short enough to repeat easily. The quality of any individual session matters less than the [Consistency]({{ '/wiki/consistency/' | relative_url }}) of doing them.

A session that ends while stability holds leaves the system in a more accessible state than it was at the start. A session that ends after pushing past the boundary leaves it in a more defended state. The difference compounds across sessions: one direction builds [Access]({{ '/wiki/access/' | relative_url }}), the other erodes it.

The session's success is measured by the condition of the system when you leave: stable means successful, regardless of how much or how little occurred. Progress does not look like breakthrough. It looks like a session that felt unremarkable but held stability throughout, followed by another session where contact was slightly easier to establish. The improvement is in the [Baseline]({{ '/wiki/baseline/' | relative_url }}), not in the peak.

The urge to push further typically appears at the point where [stability is beginning to narrow]({{ '/wiki/stable-range/' | relative_url }}). That urge is not neutral motivation. It is the most reliable indicator that the boundary is close. Exit at the boundary, not after it.

Sessions are distinct from [real-time use]({{ '/wiki/notice-step-back-adjust/' | relative_url }}). Sessions build the [Access]({{ '/wiki/access/' | relative_url }}). Real-time application is how that access gets used in the moments that matter.
