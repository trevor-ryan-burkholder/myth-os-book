A term from Internal Family Systems (IFS) therapy. A part whose function is to prevent [exiles]({{ '/wiki/exile/' | relative_url }}) from being triggered and their pain from surfacing.

IFS identifies two types:

- **Managers** are proactive. They work in advance to keep situations controlled, to prevent exiles from being triggered, and to maintain the functional appearance of stability.
- **Firefighters** are reactive. They activate after an exile has already been triggered. Their job is to suppress the pain as quickly as possible, often through behaviors that create problems of their own: substance use, dissociation, rage, compulsive action.

In Myth-OS, protector presences are those that produce closure, withdrawal, or destabilization when their defended territory is approached. They map to the same underlying structure IFS describes, named with the Myth-OS vocabulary of [presences]({{ '/wiki/presence/' | relative_url }}) and [the space]({{ '/wiki/the-space/' | relative_url }}).

In a [fragmented system]({{ '/wiki/working-with-a-fragmented-system/' | relative_url }}), protectors are often the architecture of the fragmentation itself, load-bearing walls rather than doors. The separation between parts, the structural walls that keep certain material inaccessible to other parts, is itself a protective function. You do not build a relationship with a load-bearing wall by pushing on it. You build it by demonstrating, over a long time, that you are not a structural threat.

The Myth-OS approach to protectors is the same as the approach to any [Presence]({{ '/wiki/presence/' | relative_url }}): observe without forcing, [acknowledge]({{ '/wiki/acknowledgment/' | relative_url }}) without demanding, hold position without leaning. What changes in a fragmented system is the scale of patience required and the additional [Distance]({{ '/wiki/distance/' | relative_url }}) needed. Approach starts further back than feels necessary. The first several sessions near a strongly defended protector may involve no [Acknowledgment]({{ '/wiki/acknowledgment/' | relative_url }}) at all, only being present in [The space]({{ '/wiki/the-space/' | relative_url }}) while the protector is also there.

Somatically, protectors often register as tightening, blocking sensations, or a quality of forward pressure that precedes avoidance.
