The system works because of restraint. Every step depends on keeping input low enough that the system can respond on its own terms. Misuse is not random. It follows a consistent path, and that path leads back to pressure.

Every common way the practice goes wrong is a variation of the same error: adding force to a process that stops working when forced. The forms look different from each other. The underlying structure is identical.

---

## The four distortions

**Over-control.** Trying to stop a signal from activating, change how a presence behaves through repeated pressure, or force a specific outcome. A presence is noticed and the immediate move is to change it. A signal activates and the response is to shut it down rather than observe it. The session becomes an intervention rather than a [Contact]({{ '/wiki/contact/' | relative_url }}). The outcome is predictable. The system responds the same way it responds to any demand: clarity reduces, the presence distorts or withdraws.

**Over-analysis.** Replacing observation with interpretation, assigning meaning to every signal, building explanations for every presence. The space becomes increasingly conceptual. Sessions produce more sophisticated explanations and less direct contact. The presences become ideas rather than elements that can be observed and approached. The model is coherent and detailed. It is also a replacement for the actual thing, which is no longer being accessed.

**Avoidance.** The least obvious of the four because it can look like correct use. The space is being entered, sessions are being run, the process is being followed. What is actually happening is that the internal environment is being used as a substitute for external engagement rather than a support for it. Real situations become difficult and instead of moving through them, you retreat into [The space]({{ '/wiki/the-space/' | relative_url }}) to process them symbolically. The system was built to translate internal work into real-world change. When it is used to avoid that translation, the internal work becomes self-contained.

**[Construction]({{ '/wiki/construction/' | relative_url }}).** The hardest to detect, because it does not feel like misuse. The system produces what you expect to find rather than revealing what is actually there. Sessions feel productive. The space feels rich and navigable. None of it is real. The framework becomes a projector rather than a lens.

---

## The shared underlying quality

Across all four:

- Intensity increases rather than stabilizing.
- Sessions feel more charged over time, not less.
- Outcomes are being pursued rather than observed.
- Interpretation is constant: meaning is being assigned rather than allowed to emerge.
- Real situations are being avoided rather than engaged.
- Material arrives with a quality of confirmation rather than discovery.

Any of these indicates that pressure has re-entered the process. The source does not matter. Pressure produces the same outcome regardless of its origin.

---

## The reset

The reset is mechanical. When any warning sign is present, the correction is the same:

- Reduce intensity.
- Stop forcing interaction.
- Return to the basic process: observe without engaging, hold the space without entering with an agenda, make light contact and exit cleanly.

Do not try to understand what went wrong before returning to the process. Analysis of the misuse is itself a form of over-analysis. The correction is behavioral, not conceptual.

---

## The misuse check

Five questions, applied briefly after any session that felt effortful or produced a sense of having gone backward:

- *Am I trying to control this?*
- *Am I adding pressure?*
- *Am I avoiding something real?*
- *Am I analyzing instead of observing?*
- *Is what I'm finding shaped by what I expected to find?*

If the answer to any of these is yes, the correction is not a new technique or a deeper insight. It is a return to observation, stability, and [minimal input]({{ '/wiki/minimal-input/' | relative_url }}), the same conditions that made the system work in the first place.
