The window of arousal within which the system can function. Not baseline calm. The functional zone.

The stable range is the conditions under which the system remains open and accessible during a session. Within it, interaction is possible. Outside it, access closes. The stable range expands through repeated stable contact and contracts through destabilization.

The urge to push further typically appears at the point where the stable range is beginning to narrow, which makes it an exit signal rather than an invitation. The urge to push has the quality of opportunity. What it actually indicates is that intensity is increasing and the stable range is narrowing. Following the urge confirms the instability it was signaling.

Stopping early is not leaving something unfinished. It is the correct action at the correct moment. A session that ends while the stable range is intact leaves the system in a more accessible state than it was at the start. A session that ends after pushing past the boundary leaves it in a more defended state.

In a [fragmented system]({{ '/wiki/working-with-a-fragmented-system/' | relative_url }}), the exit criteria are not guidelines. They are stops. Rising intensity: exit. Clarity dropping: exit. Body signaling escalating pressure: exit. The urge to push further arrives earlier in a dissociative system and is less reliable as a signal of genuine opportunity.
