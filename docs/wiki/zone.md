An area of [The space](/wiki/the-space/) that feels qualitatively distinct from adjacent areas.

Zones are recognized through observation, not imposed through categorization. The distinction between zones creates the boundary structure that prevents intensity from spreading across the whole space. Identifying where the space feels most stable provides the starting position for sessions.

One area may feel stable and easy to remain in. Another may feel unclear, charged, or harder to hold attention near. A third may feel empty. These distinctions are not categories you impose, they are differences you notice. The stable area does not become stable because you decide it is. It feels stable because that is how it presents when you observe it.

Boundaries follow from zones. Once you notice that two areas feel different, a boundary already exists between them. Recognizing it explicitly does one thing: it prevents the areas from bleeding into each other. An unstable area stays contained when you can tell where it ends.

Zones are also a lever for [Influence](/wiki/influence/). The same [Signal](/wiki/signal/) behaves differently depending on where in the space it is occurring. A signal that is intense in an unstable zone often becomes more manageable when observed from a neutral area.
