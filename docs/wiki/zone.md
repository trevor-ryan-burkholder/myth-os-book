An area of [The space](/wiki/the-space/) that feels qualitatively distinct from adjacent areas.

Zones are recognized through observation, not imposed through categorization. The distinction between zones produces the boundary structure that prevents intensity from spreading across the whole space. Identifying where the space feels most stable provides the starting position for sessions.

One area feels stable and easy to remain in. Another feels unclear, charged, or harder to hold attention near. A third feels empty. These are not categories you impose. They are differences you notice. The stable area is not stable because you decided it is. It feels stable because that is how it presents under observation.

Boundaries follow from zones. Once you notice two areas feel different, a boundary already exists. Recognizing it explicitly does one thing: it prevents the areas from bleeding into each other. An unstable area stays contained when you tell where it ends.

Zones are also a lever for [Influence](/wiki/influence/). The same [Signal](/wiki/signal/) behaves differently depending on where in the space it is occurring. A signal intense in an unstable zone often becomes manageable when observed from a neutral area.
