The floor level of awareness and access available under ordinary conditions.

Baseline is established through [Consistency](/wiki/consistency/) and represents the minimum available when conditions are not ideal. A higher baseline means faster signal recognition, easier access to [The space](/wiki/the-space/), and more reliable real-time use without deliberate effort. Baseline is a floor, not a ceiling. It drops under stress and rises through repetition.

The system adapts to what it encounters repeatedly under stable conditions. A single intense session followed by days of nothing leaves the system roughly where it was. Intensity produces a temporary shift. It does not change what the system treats as normal, because normal is set by what keeps happening, not by what happened once.

Baseline forms gradually. Within weeks to months of consistent short sessions, [Detection](/wiki/detection/) becomes faster, the [Somatic channel](/wiki/somatic-channel/) settles into [The space](/wiki/the-space/) with less preparation, and contact that required setup becomes available with less overhead. The marker is that you notice things without having tried. If you are trying to notice, the system has not yet made that move.

In a [fragmented system](/wiki/working-with-a-fragmented-system/), baseline is part-specific. The floor available to the part currently present varies. A day when the floor seems lower may be a day when a less-practiced part is fronting, not a sign that anything has been undone.
