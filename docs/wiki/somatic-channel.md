The body's parallel feedback system during internal work, running alongside and often preceding visual or spatial perception of [The space](/wiki/the-space/).

The somatic channel registers [Approach](/wiki/approach/), [Contact](/wiki/contact/), escalating pressure, and successful [Influence](/wiki/influence/) before cognitive processing catches up. It is co-equal with, not secondary to, the visual channel. Learning to read somatic signals gives more lead time for exit decisions and more reliable assessment of contact quality.

Some people find the somatic channel more immediately available than imagery. Others find it secondary. Both are valid access routes and both carry real information. Use whichever arrives more clearly.

The body responds to leaning before the mind notices the lean. If you are moving toward a [Presence](/wiki/presence/) with [demand](/wiki/defenses/), you will often feel it physically before you notice it cognitively: a forward quality in the chest, a pulling sensation, a change in breathing that precedes the impulse to move closer. Catching it somatically gives you more room to hold the approach correctly.

The somatic version of [stepping back](/wiki/notice-step-back-adjust/) in real-time use is a slight internal settling, a quality of the body becoming less fused with the signal's momentum. It can be as small as a single exhale slightly longer than the previous one, or a momentary attending to physical ground.

In [fragmented systems](/wiki/working-with-a-fragmented-system/), somatic grounding moves are cross-state transferable: the body responds to them before the part present has cognitive access to the practice. This makes them the only available real-time move when a less-practiced part is active.
