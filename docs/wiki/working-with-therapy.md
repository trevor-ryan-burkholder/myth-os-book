Myth-OS and therapy are not competing. They operate at different levels through different mechanisms and run simultaneously without interference, provided the distinction is held clearly.

---

## What each provides

**Therapy** (particularly IFS, somatic work, EMDR, psychodynamic) involves a relational container. The therapeutic relationship is part of the mechanism. A trained professional tracks your state in real time, adjusts pacing based on what they observe, and provides co-regulation that makes contact with highly charged material possible for people who cannot yet access that stability independently. Myth-OS does not provide that and does not attempt to.

**Myth-OS** provides between-session access. The stability built through consistent practice, the familiarity with specific signals, the developed capacity to hold a position without being pulled in, the lowered resistance to approaching presences — these support in-session therapeutic work. You arrive at sessions with more access, more vocabulary, and more [baseline](/wiki/baseline/) stability than you would without the practice. Therapy becomes more efficient without changing what therapy is.

## Coordination

If your therapist uses IFS or a parts-based approach, tell them. Not because of conflict. Because the terminology overlaps. What your therapist calls a part, this system calls a [Presence](/wiki/presence/). What your therapist calls a [Protector](/wiki/protector/) activation, this system calls a [Signal](/wiki/signal/) pointing to a presence. Same underlying territory. Different vocabularies create confusion. Consistent language prevents that without requiring your therapist to adopt the framework.

If your therapist does not use IFS or parts-based work, the practice runs in parallel without coordination. You are building access and familiarity between sessions. What you bring to therapy will be richer for it, whether or not your therapist knows where it came from.

## Bringing practice material into therapy

Describe what you observed. Not what you concluded. Your therapist is equipped to work with observations. They may not be equipped to work with a framework they do not know.

*I noticed that when certain things happen, something in me shuts down quickly and I cannot access what I was feeling* is an observation.

*My protector presence is blocking access to an exile* is a framework conclusion that requires your therapist to either adopt your framework or work around it.

The observation is more useful in any therapeutic context.

## When therapy is not available

Myth-OS was developed in part because therapy is not always available, because of cost, geography, availability, or the limits of what therapy reaches between sessions. For people in that position, the practice is designed to be sufficient for the work it describes. Not a replacement for therapy when therapy is what is needed. An independent practice that does what it does whether or not therapy is running alongside it.

If the conditions in [When to stop](/wiki/when-to-stop/) are present and therapy is not available, the priority is stabilization through whatever means are accessible: crisis lines, community support, grounding practices, external structure. Not this practice. The practice requires a minimum of stability to function. Building that minimum is the work before the work.

---

## When the practice surfaces material that exceeds independent capacity

Done consistently over time, the practice eventually surfaces material the current level of stability does not hold safely. Not because something has gone wrong. Because the practice has reached what it was building toward.

The signals are clear. Sessions that were manageable begin producing escalating intensity that is hard to contain. Aftermath becomes more difficult than the sessions themselves. In a [fragmented system](/wiki/working-with-a-fragmented-system/), switching increases around sessions.

This is the system indicating that what has become accessible requires more support than independent practice provides. Not a failure of the practice. An accurate signal about what the material needs. The practice builds access. For the most significant material, that access eventually requires a container the practice alone does not provide.
