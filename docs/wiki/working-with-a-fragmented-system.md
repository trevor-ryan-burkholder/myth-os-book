The conditions under which Myth-OS is used by someone with DID, OSDD, or significant dissociative responses to trauma.

The principles do not change in a fragmented system. [Indirect access](/wiki/resonance/), [stability before depth](/wiki/stable-range/), [minimal input](/wiki/minimal-input/), no forcing, these remain the operating principles. In fact they become more important, not less, because the consequences of violating them are faster and more complete. The system can switch states, fragment further, or produce flooding that is harder to contain when the underlying structure is already separated.

What changes is the relationship between sessions, the architecture of the protector system, and the timeline.

---

## What changes

**Each session may need to begin from scratch.** Not because previous sessions did not happen, but because the part present may not have access to them. The opening moves of each session, [detecting](/wiki/detection/) [The space](/wiki/the-space/), locating the structure, checking what is present, are not formalities to rush through. They are genuine first contact with conditions that may have changed since the last session, conducted by a part that may be encountering them for the first time.

**The space may look different depending on who is in it.** Some parts access [The space](/wiki/the-space/) as a structured environment with familiar zones and recognizable presences. Others encounter it as darker, more chaotic, less navigable. Others have no access at all. This is normal for a dissociative system and does not indicate that the work is not working. It indicates which parts have developed familiarity with the space and which have not.

**Presences may be unknown to the part currently working.** In an integrated system, a [Presence](/wiki/presence/) encountered over multiple sessions becomes familiar. In a fragmented system, a part conducting a session may encounter presences that other parts have worked with extensively but that this part has never seen before. Treat it as first [Contact](/wiki/contact/).

---

## Protectors as load-bearing walls

In a fragmented system, [protectors](/wiki/protector/) are often the reason the fragmentation exists. The separation between parts, the structural walls that keep certain material inaccessible to other parts, is itself a protective function. The system did not fragment randomly. It fragmented because the material being carried was more than a unified system could hold without destabilizing everything.

These protectors are not testing whether you are safe enough to let in. They are maintaining a structure that does not operate on that logic. They are not gatekeepers who can be convinced. They are load-bearing walls. You do not build a relationship with a load-bearing wall by pushing on it. You build it by demonstrating, over a long time, that you are not a structural threat.

## Continuity across states

The absence of automatic continuity does not mean continuity is impossible. It requires deliberate cultivation rather than being assumed.

- **External records** of sessions. Brief notes: what part was present, what area of the space was accessible, whether anything was encountered, what the body registered. These are available across states in a way that internal memory may not be.
- **[Resonance vectors](/wiki/resonance-vector/)** as cross-state anchors. The resonance response operates at a level the dissociative system does not fully separate. A piece of music that produces a settling response across states is a cross-state stabilization resource.
- **[Somatic grounding](/wiki/somatic-channel/)** moves are similarly cross-state transferable. The body responds to them before the part present has cognitive access to the practice.

---

## Stability as the primary goal

In an integrated system, the [stability principles](/wiki/stable-range/) function as good practice. In a fragmented system, they function as structural requirements. The exit criteria are not guidelines. They are stops. Rising intensity: exit. Clarity dropping: exit. Body signaling escalating pressure: exit. The urge to push further arrives earlier in a dissociative system and is less reliable as a signal of genuine opportunity.

Systemic stability has an additional dimension: the system as a whole, across parts, not only the part currently active, needs to not be in a state of active crisis, significant flooding, or escalating internal conflict. Increased switching, unusual reactivity in daily life, sleep disruption, somatic symptoms without obvious cause, are often the only visible indicators that something is happening in parts not currently in contact.

---

## Real-time use

The [real-time loop](/wiki/notice-step-back-adjust/) in a fragmented system adds a second question to *this is that signal*: *which part am I right now, and does this part have access to the practice?* When the answer is that the current part has no access, the somatic step alone is what remains available, the exhale, the physical contact with ground.

---

## Timeline

Progress in a fragmented system is longer and less linear than in an integrated one. There are periods of genuine development followed by periods of regression. Holding the timeline accurately, often years rather than months, prevents the chronic overreach that comes from expecting faster progress than the system can sustain.
