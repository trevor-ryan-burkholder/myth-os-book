The natural development of the symbolic system over time as the practice deepens.

[Resonance vectors](/wiki/resonance-vector/) shift in quality as [presences](/wiki/presence/) become more or less accessible. New symbols produce resonance that did not before. Presences change in their relationship to approach and contact. Evolution is observed, not managed. It happens through correct practice, not through redesigning the system.

Vectors that pointed at a heavily defended presence early in the practice often keep pointing there for years. Vectors that pointed at accessible material may shift elsewhere once that material has been worked with enough that the alignment moves. The system is pointing at different territory because different territory has become relevant.

Presences also change. A presence that produced immediate shutdown on approach — any move toward it triggering rapid closure — may, after extended stable [Contact](/wiki/contact/) at appropriate [Distance](/wiki/distance/), allow observation from closer range. The [Stable range](/wiki/stable-range/) has expanded. Not because the presence has changed its essential character, but because the accumulated record of contact without harm has reduced the defensive charge around it.

The space itself develops. Areas indistinct early in the practice become differentiated. New zones become accessible.

The instinct when the system begins to change is to manage the change, to guide it toward a particular configuration. Set that instinct aside. It is the same instinct as forcing [Contact](/wiki/contact/) with a [Presence](/wiki/presence/) not yet accessible. Evolution happens through the practice, not through managing the practice.

The Jungian framework gives the most developed account: see [Individuation](/wiki/individuation/) and [Archetype](/wiki/archetype/).
