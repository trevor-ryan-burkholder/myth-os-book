A term from Jungian psychology. The lifelong process of psychological development toward wholeness, the gradual integration of aspects of the self that have been rejected, suppressed, or undeveloped.

Individuation is not a goal that can be achieved and checked off. It is a direction of development that continues as long as the person is alive and growing. It is not about becoming a different person. It is about becoming more fully who you already are, bringing into relationship with consciousness the material that has been operating outside of it.

The [Symbolic evolution](/wiki/symbolic-evolution/) described in Chapter 19, and the long arc of the practice described in Chapter 20, are consistent with the individuation process without requiring its theoretical framework. The trajectory the practice produces, presences that were once defended becoming more accessible, the stable range expanding, the system gradually changing as more of its material is encountered and worked with, is Jungian individuation operationalized as a practical observation.

Myth-OS does not produce individuation by design. It builds the conditions under which individuation can occur by making the internal material accessible. What the practice produces, earlier [Detection](/wiki/detection/), reduced intensity, expanded [Stable range](/wiki/stable-range/), presences becoming more approachable, is the ground on which the deeper individuation work can eventually happen.
