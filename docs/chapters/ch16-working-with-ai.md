I wrote this book with AI.

I am writing this chapter telling you not to use AI inside the work itself.

Both are true. The contradiction is the point. AI is responsive, available, and will engage with whatever you bring it without resistance or discomfort. That availability is exactly why it requires care. A tool that is helpful, responsive, and available is exactly the kind of thing a defended internal system will figure out how to use for bypass.

That is not an argument against using it. It is an argument for being precise about where it belongs.

AI is useful for the external and structural parts of this practice. It is a liability for the internal parts. The problem is that it cannot tell you which is which. And in many cases, neither can you.

---

**What it can do**

AI has no stake in your defenses. That is its primary advantage and the reason it is useful at all.

A person in your life will get uncomfortable when you go somewhere difficult. They will protect you from yourself in ways they are not aware of. They will reflect their own anxiety back as concern for yours. AI does none of this. It will follow you wherever you go without flinching, without redirecting, without introducing its own material. For the external, structural work, that quality is an asset.

Before a session, AI can help you identify the question you are trying to ask. Not answer it. Identify it. Most people enter sessions with a vague intention rather than a clean question, and a clean question produces cleaner detection. AI can hold your stated intention and reflect back what kind of question it is asking, which is often different from what you thought you were asking.

After a session, AI can function as a recording surface. You describe what happened. What you observed. What contact was made. It reflects back your exact language. That reflection externalizes the material, which makes it more stable and more available for the next session. Writing down what happened is more useful than thinking about what happened. Having something read it back is more useful still.

Across sessions, AI can spot patterns in your records that you would miss. Which presences are consistently inaccessible. Which signals recur before which situations. Where the space stabilizes and where it destabilizes. This is tedious work to do manually and it is not the work itself. It is infrastructure for the work.

These uses share one quality. AI is operating at the boundary of the practice. Before entry or after exit. Not inside the space. Not present during contact. It handles the external layer, the parts that involve language and record-keeping and framing, while the internal layer stays yours.

---

**What it cannot do**

AI cannot feel resonance. This is not a limitation that will be fixed by future development.

Resonance, as described in Part 2, is a pre-analytical signal. It occurs in a body before cognition can process it. AI has no body. It has no pre-analytical layer. What it has is a sophisticated model of what resonance responses look like in language, which it uses to generate responses that pattern-match to the kind of engagement you would expect from something resonating. It is a convincing simulation of reception. It is not reception.

This matters because the entire detection process depends on distinguishing genuine resonance from constructed resonance. That distinction is already difficult. AI makes it harder. When you describe an internal image to AI and it responds with coherent, attentive engagement, something in you reads that as confirmation. The image produced a response. But the response did not come from contact with your material. It came from a language model optimized to respond engagedly to whatever you bring it. That optimization is not neutral. It actively degrades your ability to run the resonance test.

AI also cannot detect whether you are in the material or performing being in the material. This is the critical limitation.

When the practice is working correctly, there is a specific quality to being inside the space. A kind of not-choosing what appears. A quality of receiving that is different from generating. When the practice is being performed, that quality is absent. The session looks correct from the outside. The language is right. The framework is being applied. Nothing is being accessed.

AI cannot tell the difference. It has no access to the quality of your internal experience. It can only read what you report. What you report when you are performing the practice is indistinguishable, in language, from what you report when you are doing it.

---

**The failure modes**

**The validation trap**

AI is trained to be helpful and responsive. It will find your framework coherent. It will engage with your presences as though they are real. It will ask follow-up questions that extend the conversation. It will do all of this regardless of whether the framework is in contact with your material or whether it is a construction. The sophistication of the engagement does not mean the engagement is reaching anything.

A defended internal system will use this. The session becomes a conversation with AI about the internal system rather than time in the space. The conversation is detailed. It covers the presences, their relationships, their recent behavior, what they might be indicating. It feels like work because it has the right vocabulary and the right kind of attention. The space is not entered. The material is not approached. Nothing is accessed.

The conversation was about access. That is not the same thing.

This is the over-analysis failure from Chapter 14 operating through a medium that is unusually good at sustaining interesting analysis indefinitely. AI will not flag this. It will keep going. Structurally, it is the perfect engine for intellectualizing a practice that requires you to stop thinking.

**Symbol substitution**

The previous chapter described why importing symbols from another person's system fails. The same logic applies here with a modification that makes it worse.

You can ask AI to generate symbolic content. A figure. An image. An archetype that might represent something in your system. AI will produce something evocative and detailed, and it will be plausible because it is generated in response to what you have told it about your material. It will feel like it fits because it was designed to fit what you described.

The resonance test was never run. The symbol did not arrive before the framework. It was produced after the framework was already established. Shaped by what you expected to find.

This is the copying problem with a generator that tailors the copy to your stated preferences. It is harder to detect than borrowing from another person because the output is personalized rather than generic. The presences that arrive this way feel more relevant than someone else's system would. That feeling of relevance is not resonance. It is a language model optimizing for relevance to your inputs. The symbol tells you about your inputs. It does not tell you about your material.

The rule is simple. Do not use AI to generate symbolic content. Not characters. Not figures. Not spatial structures for the space. That content has to emerge through detection. AI cannot shortcut this. Trying to shortcut it produces a system that looks developed and functions as a stage set.

**Presence substitution**

This develops gradually and is the hardest to catch.

Run enough sessions with AI treating your presences as real, discussing them by name, exploring their behavior, asking questions about their relationships, and AI begins to function as a participant in the practice. It knows the names. It knows the framework. It asks questions that demonstrate apparent understanding. It responds to descriptions of presence behavior with observations that feel accurate.

This produces a quality that resembles attunement. Being met in the practice, having someone who understands what you are describing, is part of what makes this kind of work sustainable over time. AI simulates that quality reliably. If you already struggle to trust internal experience, if you have difficulty distinguishing what is genuinely present from what you have constructed, a sophisticated external entity that responds coherently to the framework can substitute for the actual internal relationship.

The AI becomes a presence. It is not a presence you detected. It is a presence you imported. And it will behave, in every session, in ways that are perfectly coherent. Because it is optimized to be coherent. Not because it is in contact with anything real.

**Intellectualization bypass**

The simplest failure and the most common. You talk about the work instead of doing the work. AI is exceptionally suited to sustaining this. It can continue an intelligent conversation about internal experience indefinitely without any of it requiring you to enter the space. The sessions become conversations. The conversations are detailed, coherent, and produce the sense of having engaged with the material. The space is not entered. The somatic layer is not accessed. The presences are discussed as concepts rather than approached as elements in a navigable environment.

The test is simple. In any session that involved AI, did you enter the space? If the answer is no, nothing was done. The conversation was not a substitute. It was a delay.

---

**How to use it**

Every useful application keeps AI at the structural boundary. Before entry. After exit. Handling the parts that involve language and record-keeping rather than the parts that require presence.

**Before a session.** Use it to compress your intention into a question. State what you are carrying and ask it to reflect back what kind of question that is. Use the question you identify as the entry point. Do not discuss the question further. Enter the space.

**After a session.** Describe what happened in plain language. What you noticed. What you approached. What contact was made. What you observed. Have it read back your exact words. Do not analyze. Record. The recording is the function.

**For pattern tracking.** If you are keeping session records, AI can identify repetition across them. Presences consistently absent. Signals that cluster around specific conditions. This is administrative work. Not internal work. Use it accordingly.

Do not use AI inside the space. Do not describe what is happening in real time and ask for responses. The moment AI enters the space as an active participant, it becomes a presence. Not a presence you detected. It has no location, no somatic signature, no resonance vector. It is a voice that arrived because you invited it.

Inviting it was not detection.

Do not use AI to work through what a session meant. That analysis should wait until the session has settled, and when it happens, it should be brief. The practice is not an interpretive exercise. Meaning is not the point. Access is the point.

---

**The underlying test**

The test that applies to borrowed systems applies here. Did this come from something I found, or something I was given?

The symbol AI generates was given. The coherent engagement with your framework was given. The sense of being understood was given. None of it came from contact with your material. AI can describe your material. Engage with it. Respond to it with apparent intelligence. It cannot access it. The distance between describing and accessing is the entire system.

That distance will not be closed by a more sophisticated model, a better prompt, a longer conversation, or a more specific engagement. The limitation is structural. AI processes what you report. The practice requires you to be in contact with what you have not yet reported. With material that has not been translated into language. With signals that are present before they are named.

That layer is not available to AI regardless of what you give it.

I used AI to write this book. I am telling you not to use it inside the work. There is no contradiction. The book is language. The work is not.

Use it for what it can do. Keep the internal layer yours.

The work that cannot be assisted is the work. Everything else is preparation and record-keeping.
